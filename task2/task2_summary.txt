
EDA was performed on the Titanic dataset containing 891 passenger records.

Key Findings:
1. Female passengers had a significantly higher survival rate than male passengers.
2. Passengers in higher classes generally paid higher fares and experienced better survival rates.
3. Most passengers were between 20 and 40 years old.

The Fare variable contains several outliers, indicating a few passengers paid exceptionally high ticket prices.
Correlation analysis showed that passenger class and fare have noticeable relationships with survival.
