
Exploratory Data Analysis was performed on the Titanic dataset containing 891 passenger records.
Missing values were identified and handled using median and mode imputation, while cabin values were replaced with 'Unknown'.
Duplicates were removed and a new feature called FamilySize was created.

Three major findings were obtained from the analysis.
First, passengers in higher classes had better survival rates than those in lower classes.
Second, most passengers were between 20 and 40 years old.
Third, passengers with smaller families generally had a higher probability of survival.

Correlation analysis showed relationships between survival, passenger class and fare.
Various visualizations such as bar charts, histograms, scatter plots and heatmaps were used to understand the data.
The cleaned dataset and visualizations provide meaningful insights into factors affecting passenger survival.
